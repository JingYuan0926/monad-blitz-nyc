### Vitalik Buterin

#### Early Life / Background
- Born January 31, 1994, in Kolomna, Russia; his father, Dmitry Buterin, is a computer scientist who later introduced him to Bitcoin.
- Emigrated with his parents to Canada at age 6 (around 2000) in search of better employment opportunities.
- Placed in a class for gifted children in grade three; drawn early to mathematics, programming, and economics.
- Discovered Bitcoin in 2011 at age 17 and began experimenting with blockchain code; co-founded *Bitcoin Magazine* the same year, writing extensively about cryptocurrency.
- Dropped out of the University of Waterloo in 2014 after receiving a $100,000 grant from the Thiel Fellowship (Peter Thiel's program paying students to leave school and build companies).

#### Career Timeline — Founding Ethereum & The Merge
- **Late 2013 (age 19):** Wrote the Ethereum whitepaper, conceiving a blockchain that went beyond payments to support smart contracts and decentralized applications (dApps).
- **January 2014:** Publicly unveiled Ethereum at a Bitcoin meetup/conference in Miami.
- **Mid-2014:** Ether crowdsale — investors could buy ETH for as little as ~$0.31 apiece; the sale collected over 31,000 BTC (roughly €18 million at the time).
- **July 2015:** Ethereum blockchain launched, with co-founders including Gavin Wood, Charles Hoskinson, Anthony Di Iorio, and Joseph Lubin.
- **September 15, 2022 — The Merge:** Ethereum Mainnet merged with the proof-of-stake Beacon Chain, ending energy-intensive mining. Energy consumption fell by ~99.95% (a Consensys-cited report put the carbon-footprint reduction at 99.99%). Buterin tweeted that day: "The merge will reduce worldwide electricity consumption by 0.2%."
- **December 3, 2025:** Fusaka hard fork went live, introducing PeerDAS (Peer Data Availability Sampling) — a step toward Danksharding — with expected Layer-2 fee reductions of 40–60%. Next major upgrade, Glamsterdam, expected in H1 2026.

#### Numbers & Records
- **Net worth (estimates only — almost entirely on-chain ETH, so highly price-dependent):**
  - Early/May 2026: estimated between ~$467 million and ~$750 million (Arkham Intelligence and related trackers).
  - One June 2026 source estimates "at least $380 million" amid ETH's slide to roughly $1,700.
  - Peaked around ~$1.2 billion in August 2025 when ETH neared a ~$5,000 all-time high.
  - Holdings estimated at ~224,000–240,000 ETH as of May 2026, down from ~330,000–333,500 ETH in 2021 (reduced largely via donations).
- **May 2021:** Named the world's youngest crypto billionaire at age 27 (his ~333,500 ETH topped $1.1 billion when ETH hit $3,500).
- **2021:** Named to TIME's 100 Most Influential People (Innovators category, alongside Elon Musk and Jensen Huang).
- **X/Twitter followers (@VitalikButerin):** ~5.2 million as of March 2024; more recent listings show ~6.7 million (estimate — exact current count varies by source).
- **2022:** Granted Montenegrin citizenship at the suggestion of PM Zdravko Krivokapić and the initiative of finance minister Milojko Spajić, as part of Montenegro's bid to become a blockchain hub.

#### Philanthropy
- **May 2021:** After Shiba Inu's creators sent him half the SHIB supply, donated roughly $1 billion worth of SHIB to India's CryptoRelief COVID-19 fund — one of the largest individual crypto donations ever — and burned most of the remainder.
- **January 2022:** CryptoRelief returned $100 million in USDC from that donation; Buterin redeployed it to COVID science/biotech projects worldwide.
- Founded **Kanro**, a biosecurity/anti-infectious-disease charity focused on pandemic preparedness; in January 2025 he sold meme tokens sent to his wallet for ~$2.5 million and donated proceeds to Kanro.
- Made a $9.4 million USDC donation to fund medical research (via The Giving Block); has also sold ~$30 million in ETH to fund the Ethereum Foundation and biotech charity work.
- Pattern noted by analysts: increasingly uses his wealth for ecosystem and public-health support rather than personal liquidity.

#### Recent (2024–2026): Research Focus & Ethereum Foundation
- **d/acc ("defensive/decentralized/democratic/differential accelerationism"):** Introduced in his November 2023 essay "My techno-optimism"; published the retrospective "d/acc: one year later" on January 5, 2025. Advocates accelerating defense-favoring tech (pandemic preparedness, cyber defense, info defense) and human-AI collaboration over fully autonomous AI; discussed it on the 80,000 Hours podcast; registered a d/acc ENS domain.
- **May 2025:** Proposed "partially stateless nodes" — nodes keeping only data needed for their tasks — to slash disk requirements and let more users run nodes locally.
- **2025 privacy roadmap:** Write-privacy via client-side ZK-SNARKs, privacy pools, encrypted voting, and mixnet relays; read-privacy via Oblivious RAM (ORAM) and Private Information Retrieval (PIR) so RPC providers can't see user access patterns.
- **November 2025 (Devconnect Buenos Aires, ~17,000 attendees):** Outlined the 2025–2027 roadmap and "Lean Ethereum" — stripping legacy code, quantum resistance, ZK-friendly hash functions, formal verification of the base layer, and a goal of consumer smartphones running full nodes within ~two years; tied to the "Trustless Manifesto" ("trustlessness is the thing itself").
- **2026 "cypherpunk reset":** Declared 2026 the year Ethereum reclaims its cypherpunk roots, prioritizing Helios light clients and ZK-EVMs to remove reliance on trusted third-party data providers; January 1, 2026 CoinDesk interview laid out the two goals Ethereum must meet to become the "world computer."
- **Ethereum Foundation restructuring:** Hsiao-Wei Wang and Tomasz Stańczak became co-executive directors in March 2025; Stańczak stepped down at end of February 2026 (Bastian Aue interim co-ED). At least nine senior contributors departed in 2026 (incl. Tim Beiko, Barnabé Monnot, Trent Van Epps). In May 2026 Buterin defended a "smaller ship" EF: fewer ETH sales, "longevity over breadth," focus on **CROPS** (Censorship Resistance, Openness, Privacy, Security), and said his own personal authority will diminish as decentralized governance matures.

#### Fun Facts
- Played World of Warcraft from 2007–2010; when Blizzard nerfed his warlock's Siphon Life spell, he said he realized "what horrors centralized services can bring" — an oft-cited origin story for his decentralization ethos.
- His X account was hijacked via a SIM-swap attack (2023), which he publicly dissected as a security lesson.
- Adopted a Milady NFT as his X profile picture (trending in January 2025).
- Known for an ascetic lifestyle and nomadic travel despite his wealth; his net worth is unusually transparent because his wallets are publicly traceable on-chain.
- Famously said of his own invention: "When I came up with Ethereum, my first thought was, 'Okay, this thing is too good to be true.'"

#### Notable Quotes

- "In the long run, security is the ultimate scalability." — Twitter thread, 2019
- "The traditional finance world is full of rent-seeking middlemen and inefficient processes." — Quartz interview, 2018
- "My goal was never to launch an ICO." — Fortune interview, 2018

### Kartik Talwar (ETHGlobal)

#### Identification

- "Kartik Network" is **not** a blockchain project or company — no network by that name exists in indexed sources. The phrase almost certainly refers to **Kartik Talwar, co-founder of ETHGlobal**, the dominant Ethereum hackathon organization (other blockchain Kartiks — Kartik Nayak of Duke/a16z crypto research, Kartik Hegadekatti of the Govt. of India — are researchers, not "network" figures). All content below is about **Kartik Talwar**.

#### Identity & Current Roles

- **Kartik Talwar** — Co-Founder of **ETHGlobal** (co-founded October 2017 with Liam Horne) and General Partner at **A.Capital Ventures** (since December 2018).
- X/Twitter: **@TheRealKartik** (also holds @kartiktalwar). Active speaker/emcee at ETHGlobal events worldwide; listed speaker at CoinDesk's Consensus 2025.
- Best known as the public face of Ethereum's hackathon ecosystem — he typically opens and closes ETHGlobal hackathons and hosts recurring fireside chats with Vitalik Buterin.

#### Early Life / Background

- Studied **Astrophysics and Mathematics at the University of Waterloo** (2012–2016, BSc '16); was an API developer and teaching assistant in computational physics (2013), and wrote an API for the university while still a student.
- Prolific open-source author on GitHub from his student years.
- **Co-founded Hack the North**, Canada's biggest hackathon, after meeting Liam Horne and Kevin Lau in **October 2013** via a random Facebook group brainstorming a regional hackathon; the trio came from three different Waterloo faculties (science, math, engineering) and had been "enthralled" attending some of the first US collegiate hackathons in 2012. The first edition drew ~1,000 students for 36 hours.

### Jensen Huang

#### Early Life / Background
- Born Jen-Hsun "Jensen" Huang on February 17, 1963, in Tainan, Taiwan; family relocated to Thailand when he was about five years old.
- Sent to the U.S. at age nine (1973) with his older brother to live with an aunt and uncle in Tacoma, Washington.
- His relatives mistakenly enrolled the boys in the Oneida Baptist Institute in Oneida, Kentucky — a religious reform academy for troubled youth — believing it was a prestigious boarding school. Too young for the academy's classes, Jensen attended Oneida Elementary, where he was frequently bullied as an undersized immigrant with accented English.
- At Oneida he taught his illiterate 17-year-old roommate — "covered in tattoos and knife scars" — to read, in exchange for lessons on bench pressing.
- Parents later immigrated and settled in Beaverton, Oregon; the brothers rejoined them after roughly two years in Kentucky.
- As a teenager, became a nationally ranked table-tennis player; also a member of math, computer, and science clubs.
- Worked the graveyard shift at a local Denny's as a dishwasher, busboy, and waiter starting at age 15 (roughly 1978–1983) — a job he still cites as formative.
- B.S. in electrical engineering, Oregon State University, 1984 (graduated with highest honors; chose OSU for its low in-state tuition). Met his wife, Lori, there as his engineering lab partner.
- M.S. in electrical engineering, Stanford University, 1992 — earned through night classes while working full-time as a chip designer (at AMD, then LSI Logic) in Silicon Valley.
- Family connection: AMD CEO Lisa Su is his relative — Huang's mother is the youngest sister of Su's maternal grandfather (first cousins, once removed).

#### NVIDIA Founding Story (The Denny's Origin)
- In 1992, Huang (then at LSI Logic) met repeatedly with engineers Chris Malachowsky and Curtis Priem (both from Sun Microsystems) at a Denny's in East San Jose to draft a business plan. Huang picked Denny's because of his prior employment there and because it was "quieter than home and had cheap coffee."
- NVIDIA was formally founded April 5, 1993. Huang officially joined on February 17, 1993 — his 30th birthday.
- Incorporation anecdote: lawyer James Gaither of Cooley Godward took the $200 cash in Huang's pocket to capitalize the company; each co-founder put in $200, making NVIDIA's initial capital $600. They started with about $40,000 in the bank.
- Though the youngest of the three, Huang was made CEO from day one. Priem: "we basically deferred to Jensen on day one."
- Near-death experience: NVIDIA's first chip, the NV1 (quadrilateral-based graphics), failed when Microsoft standardized triangle-based Direct3D in 1996; Sega abandoned the follow-on NV2 chip intended for its next console. Huang laid off roughly half the staff and the company was weeks from missing payroll.
- Sega of America's CEO Shoichiro Irimajiri provided a $5 million lifeline investment despite the failed chip — buying NVIDIA about six more months of life.
- The desperate bet: the RIVA 128 (NV3), designed in roughly nine months using emulation software, launched 1997 and sold ~1 million units in four months, saving the company. This era spawned Huang's permanent unofficial company motto: "Our company is thirty days from going out of business."
- NVIDIA IPO'd in January 1999 and invented/coined the modern "GPU" with the GeForce 256 later that year.

#### GPU / AI Dominance
- 2006: launched CUDA, a software platform letting researchers program GPUs for general-purpose parallel computing. It was expensive and didn't benefit gaming customers, but Huang subsidized it for years on the conviction that parallel computing would dominate every serious computational field.
- 2012 inflection point: AlexNet — Geoffrey Hinton's students Alex Krizhevsky and Ilya Sutskever — won ImageNet with a 15.3% error rate (vs. 26.2% for second place), trained on two NVIDIA GTX 580 GPUs using CUDA. The deep learning revolution ran on NVIDIA hardware from that moment.
- NVIDIA's data center segment grew from a tiny fraction of revenue to ~91.5% of total revenue; the company commands an estimated 90%+ share of data-center discrete GPUs and powers an estimated 90%+ of cloud AI workloads.
- Architecture roadmap: Blackwell (2024–25) → Vera Rubin platform (2026: Rubin R100 GPU with 336 billion transistors plus custom 88-core Vera CPU; claimed ~3.3x Blackwell compute, 5x inference performance) → Rubin Ultra (2027) → Feynman announced on the roadmap.

#### Numbers & Records
- Market cap milestones: $1 trillion (May 2023) → $3 trillion (June 2024) → first company ever to $4 trillion (July 9, 2025) → first to $5 trillion, reaching $5.03T on October 29, 2025. As of June 2026: ~$5.05 trillion (estimate; world's most valuable company).
- Fiscal 2026 (year ended January 25, 2026): revenue $215.9 billion, up 65% YoY; data center revenue $197.3 billion (up from $115.2B in FY2025); GAAP operating income $130.4 billion.
- FY2026 quarterly revenue ramp: Q1 $44.1B → Q2 $46.7B → Q3 $57.0B → Q4 $68.1B (Q4 up 73% YoY; Q4 data center revenue $62.3B, up 75% YoY).
- Net worth (estimates vary with NVDA stock): Bloomberg Billionaires Index ~$178 billion (May 10, 2026); other 2026 estimates range ~$165B–$200B+; Forbes has placed him around 7th-wealthiest in the world. ~97% of his wealth is NVIDIA stock; he owns roughly 3.3–3.5% of the company per a March 2026 filing.
- Has 60 direct reports (some sources say 55) — far more than typical for a megacap CEO.

#### Leadership Style
- No one-on-one meetings: gives feedback in group settings so everyone learns from it; "I never schedule one-on-one meetings unless someone asks" (CNBC, June 2024).
- Radical information transparency — no privileged access; believes equal access to information empowers people and keeps everyone "working off the same song sheet."
- Deliberately flat org chart: 60 direct reports remove layers of middle management between him and decision-makers; reaches deep into the organization.
- Reasons aloud: spends meetings explaining how he thinks through decisions, treating it as continuous teaching.
- Philosophy of company size: "as large as necessary to do the job well, but as small as possible."
- Works seven days a week including holidays, and says he operates in a constant "state of anxiety" about going bankrupt (statements reported December 2025, including on The Joe Rogan Experience).
- Said in June 2026 that workers should be "paid as much as possible" (Fortune, June 2, 2026).

#### The Leather Jacket Persona

- Frequently wears Tom Ford; some of his jackets reportedly cost over $10,000. At CES 2025 he debuted an $8,990 lizard-embossed leather jacket that arguably upstaged the RTX 50-series announcement.
- His own explanation: "I'm happy that my wife and my daughter dress me." Also avoids wearing a watch because he prefers to "live in the moment."

- Has an NVIDIA logo tattoo on his left shoulder, inked when the stock first hit $100 (first publicly visible in an NVIDIA Instagram post, October 25, 2014); says he won't get inked again because it hurt more than expected.

As of February 2026, NVIDIA remained worried local Chinese AI-chip rivals could take over the market.

### Stephen Curry

#### Early Life / Background
- Born March 14, 1988, in Akron, Ohio — the same hometown as LeBron James — while his father, former NBA sharpshooter Dell Curry, played for the Cleveland Cavaliers; raised primarily in Charlotte, North Carolina.
- Full name Wardell Stephen Curry II; attended Charlotte Christian School, where he starred but was lightly recruited due to his slender ~160-pound frame.
- Dreamed of playing for Virginia Tech (his father's school) but was offered only a walk-on spot; instead chose Davidson College, which had aggressively recruited him since the 10th grade.
- Married to Ayesha Curry, entrepreneur, host, and two-time New York Times bestselling author; the couple co-founded the Eat. Learn. Play. Foundation.

#### The Davidson Story
- As a sophomore, carried No. 10 seed Davidson on a Cinderella run to the 2008 NCAA Tournament Elite Eight, upsetting Gonzaga, Georgetown, and Wisconsin (33 points in a 73-56 Sweet 16 win) before falling 59-57 to eventual champion Kansas, who double-teamed him on the final possession (he scored 25).
- Averaged 32.0 points, 3.5 assists, and 3.3 steals across the 2008 tournament, shooting 44.2% from three on ~13 attempts per game.
- Led the entire nation in scoring as a junior in 2008-09 at 28.6 PPG, finishing as Davidson's all-time leading scorer with more than 2,600 points before declaring for the draft.
- Selected 7th overall by the Golden State Warriors in the 2009 NBA Draft.
- Completed his BA in sociology remotely in 2022; Davidson — which requires a degree before retiring a jersey — retired his No. 30 in a ceremony on August 31, 2022.

#### Career Timeline
- **2009:** Drafted 7th overall by Golden State; has spent his entire career with the Warriors.
- **2014-15:** Wins first NBA MVP; Warriors win the 2015 championship.
- **2015-16:** Becomes the first unanimous MVP in NBA history (all 131 first-place votes); hits a record 402 three-pointers; leads the league in scoring (30.1 PPG), steals (2.14), and FT% (.908) — the first player ever to lead all three — while posting the first 50-40-90 season with 30+ PPG; Warriors go a record 73-9, surpassing the 1995-96 Bulls' 72-10.
- **2017, 2018:** Back-to-back championships (vs. Cleveland).

### Cristiano Ronaldo

#### Early Life / Background

- Full name: Cristiano Ronaldo dos Santos Aveiro; born **February 5, 1985** in Santo António, a neighbourhood of **Funchal, Madeira, Portugal**.
- Youngest child of Maria Dolores dos Santos Aveiro (a cook) and José Dinis Aveiro (a municipal gardener); grew up in poverty in Madeira.
- His second given name "Ronaldo" was chosen after then-US president **Ronald Reagan**, his father's favourite actor.
- At age **15** he was diagnosed with a racing heart condition that could have ended his career; he underwent surgery in which a laser cauterised multiple cardiac pathways, and recovered fully within days.
- Joined Sporting CP's academy as a boy (leaving Madeira for Lisbon at ~12); youth-teammates nicknamed him "abelhinha" ("little bee") for his speed — and "cry baby" for how often he wept.

#### Career Timeline (Clubs & Country)

- **Sporting CP (2002–2003):** First-team debut in August 2002 at age 17; ~5 goals in 31 appearances in his single senior season.
- **Manchester United (2003–2009):** Signed August 2003 for **£12 million** — then an English record fee for a teenager. 118 goals in 292 appearances in the first spell; won 3 Premier League titles, the FA Cup (2004), and the **2008 Champions League**; first Ballon d'Or in 2008.
- **Real Madrid (2009–2018):** Joined for a then-world-record **£80 million (€94m)**. Scored **450 goals in 438 appearances** in nine seasons — the club's all-time top scorer; won 4 Champions Leagues (2014, 2016, 2017, 2018), 2 La Liga titles, and 4 more Ballons d'Or.
- **Juventus (2018–2021):** €100 million transfer (plus ~€12m in fees) on July 10, 2018 — the highest fee ever for a player over 30; **101 goals in 134 appearances**, 2 Serie A titles (2019, 2020).
- **Manchester United, second spell (2021–2022):** Returned for ~€17 million; contract terminated by mutual consent in November 2022 after a controversial Piers Morgan interview.
- **Al-Nassr (Dec 30, 2022–present):** Free-agent move to Saudi Arabia reportedly worth ~$200M+ per year — the highest-paid footballer in history. ~99 goals in his first 111 matches (as of mid-2025). Signed a new contract to **2027** on June 26, 2025 (reported as worth $400M+, with reports of ~5% of club shares).
- **Portugal (2003–present):** All-time men's international top scorer and most-capped men's player (**143 goals in 226 caps** as of late 2025); won **Euro 2016**, the **2019 and 2025 UEFA Nations Leagues**; set for a record **sixth World Cup** in 2026.

#### Numbers & Records

- **~973 career goals** for club and country (ESPN goal tracker, 2026) — the all-time record; publicly chasing the unprecedented **1,000-goal milestone** (sources in 2026 range 971–973 depending on count/date).
- **143 international goals** — men's all-time record; **226 caps** — men's all-time record.
- **140 UEFA Champions League goals** — all-time record; **14 European Championship goals** — all-time record.
- Only player to appear at **six European Championships** (2004–2024) and the first to play at **six World Cups** (2006–2026).
- **5 Ballons d'Or** (2008, 2013, 2014, 2016, 2017) — the most for a European player.
- **~35 career trophies**, including 5 Champions Leagues, Euro 2016, 2 Nations Leagues, 3 Premier Leagues, 2 La Ligas, 2 Serie A titles, and the 2025–26 Saudi Pro League.
- **Most-followed person on Instagram**: ~**665.6 million followers** (#1 worldwide, June 2026, HypeAuditor) — even after losing 9+ million in Instagram's May 2026 bot/inactive-account purge.
- **YouTube launch records (Aug 21, 2024, "UR · Cristiano"):** 100,000 subscribers in 22 minutes and **1 million in ~90 minutes** — both official world records; 10 million in 11.5 hours; **30+ million within two days**.

#### Business Ventures & Wealth

- **First billionaire footballer:** added to the Bloomberg Billionaires Index in **October 2025** with an estimated net worth of **~$1.4 billion** (estimate), driven by tax-free Saudi salary plus endorsements.
- **Nike** deal reported at nearly **$18M/year**, plus sponsorships with Armani and Castrol — Bloomberg estimated endorsements/investments added $175M+ to his fortune.
- **CR7 brand:** clothing, underwear, fragrances, footwear (one 2024 estimate put CR7-brand revenue around $3.7M/year).
- **Pestana CR7 Hotels:** ~€75M joint venture with Pestana Group (2016); hotels in Lisbon, Madrid, New York, Marrakech, and Madeira.
- **CR7 Fitness gyms** (with Crunch Fitness) across Spain and Portugal; later integrated with the UFC Gym brand under TKO Group.
- **Insparya:** co-founded hair-transplant clinic chain in Spain and Portugal.
- **Medialivre:** ~30% shareholder in the Portuguese media group that owns Correio da Manhã and Record.
- Investor in **Whoop** (wearable fitness tech, valued ~$3.6B); past partnerships include ZujuGP and Binance.

#### Recent (2024–2026)

- **June–July 2024 (Euro 2024):** Played his record sixth Euros; in the round of 16 vs Slovenia his extra-time penalty was saved by Jan Oblak and he wept on the pitch — then converted in the shootout as Portugal advanced (they exited in the quarterfinals to France on penalties).
- **Aug 21, 2024:** Launched the "UR · Cristiano" YouTube channel with 19 videos — fastest channel ever to 1M subscribers.
- **June 8, 2025:** Scored in the **UEFA Nations League final** as Portugal beat Spain 5–3 on penalties (2–2 draw) — his second Nations League title.
- **June 26, 2025:** Signed Al-Nassr extension to 2027.
- **August 2025:** Engaged to long-term partner **Georgina Rodríguez** (a reported $3M ring).
- **Oct 8, 2025:** Declared football's **first billionaire player** by Bloomberg.
- **Nov 13, 2025:** Received the **first straight red card of his 226-cap international career** — sent off vs Ireland in Dublin for elbowing Dara O'Shea after a VAR review; FIFA gave a one-match ban (plus two suspended), keeping him free for the World Cup opener.

- **June 2026:** Named in Roberto Martínez's Portugal squad for a record sixth World Cup at age **41**; Portugal drawn in **Group K** vs Colombia, Uzbekistan, and DR Congo. The World Cup remains the one major trophy he has never won, and he has indicated 2026 will be his last.

### Jeremy Lin

#### Early Life / Background
- Full name Jeremy Shu-How Lin (林書豪); born August 23, 1988, in Torrance, California; raised in Palo Alto, CA by parents who emigrated from Taiwan. Devout Christian; faith is a recurring theme in his public statements.
- **High school:** Senior year (2005–06) captained Palo Alto High School to a 32–1 record and a 51–47 upset of nationally ranked Mater Dei for the CIF Division II state title. Named first-team All-State and Northern California D-II Player of the Year; senior averages: 15.1 pts, 7.1 ast, 6.2 reb, 5.0 stl.
- **Recruiting snub:** Received zero Division I athletic scholarship offers despite the state title — a foundational part of his underdog narrative.
- **Harvard (2006–10):** Earned an economics degree. Career: 115 games, 12.9 ppg, 4.3 rpg, 3.5 apg, 48.1% FG. Junior year (2008–09) he was the only NCAA D-I men's player ranked top-10 in his conference in scoring (17.8), rebounding (5.5), assists (4.3), steals (2.4), blocks (0.6), FG% (.502), FT% (.744) and 3P% (.400). Senior year: 16.4 pts, 4.4 reb, 4.5 ast, 2.4 stl, 1.1 blk. Two-time unanimous All-Ivy First Team (three-time all-conference overall).
- **Undrafted** in the 2010 NBA Draft; signed with his hometown Golden State Warriors in July 2010, becoming widely cited as the first American of Chinese/Taiwanese descent to play in the NBA.

#### Linsanity — February 2012, New York Knicks (the NYC story)
- **Setup:** Cut by the Warriors and then the Rockets in December 2011; the Knicks claimed him off waivers on **December 27, 2011** after an injury to Iman Shumpert, as a third-string backup behind Toney Douglas and Mike Bibby. His contract was non-guaranteed and he was reportedly days from being cut again.
- **The couch:** He was sleeping on a couch in his brother Josh's one-bedroom apartment on Manhattan's **Lower East Side** (and earlier on teammate Landry Fields' couch) because his deal only covered housing for 30 days. His later quote: "It's more comfortable than a lot of beds."
- **Feb 4, 2012:** Came off the bench for 25 points against the New Jersey Nets (vs. Deron Williams) — the spark.
- **Feb 10, 2012:** Dropped **38 points on Kobe Bryant's Lakers at Madison Square Garden** — the signature Linsanity game.
- **Feb 14, 2012:** Game-winning three-pointer with under a second left in a 90–87 win at Toronto (Valentine's Day dagger).
- **Feb 19, 2012:** 28 points and 14 assists to beat the defending-champion Dallas Mavericks.
- **The run:** Over the 9 "true Linsanity" games without injured Carmelo Anthony, Lin averaged **25.0 pts, 9.2 ast, 3.8 reb, 2.2 stl on 51% FG**, and the Knicks went **8–1**. He was the **first NBA player ever to score 20+ points with 7+ assists in each of his first five starts**; his 89 / 109 / 136 points through his first 3 / 4 / 5 career starts were each the most by any player since the 1976–77 ABA-NBA merger. Named Eastern Conference Player of the Week (games through Feb 12) and was a last-minute addition to the 2012 Rising Stars Challenge at All-Star Weekend.
- **Economic shockwave in NYC:** Madison Square Garden Co. stock hit a record high (+6.2% spike; market cap up ~$250M in February 2012 and ~$600M by July); Knicks online-store traffic/sales jumped **4,000%**; overall Knicks merchandise up 200% year-over-year; Lin had the **NBA's best-selling jersey**; MSG Network ratings rose ~70%; Knicks revenue was projected to rise $25–50M for 2012–13 and league revenue by up to $80M on his international appeal.
- **Media frenzy:** Back-to-back Sports Illustrated covers (February 2012) and a TIME (Asia) cover titled "Linsanity!" (Feb 27, 2012 issue); named to the TIME 100 in 2012. The USPTO ultimately awarded Lin the **"Linsanity" trademark** after dismissing roughly a dozen squatter applications. ESPN fired an employee over the widely condemned "Chink in the Armor" headline during the run — an early flashpoint in his racism advocacy. A documentary, *Linsanity* (dir. Evan Jackson Leong), premiered at Sundance in 2013.
- **The end:** After Anthony's return the Knicks went 2–6 and coach Mike D'Antoni resigned March 14, 2012; Lin's season ended with a torn meniscus in late March. In July 2012 Houston signed him to a backloaded 3-year, ~$25M offer sheet the Knicks declined to match — ending Linsanity in New York after roughly 26 games.

#### NBA Career Timeline & Numbers
- **Teams (9 seasons, 8 franchises):** Warriors (2010–11), Knicks (2011–12), Rockets (2012–14), Lakers (2014–15), Hornets (2015–16), Nets (2016–17, 3-yr/$36M deal; ruptured patellar tendon on opening night of 2017–18), Hawks (2018–19), Raptors (Feb–Jun 2019).
- **Career NBA totals:** 480 regular-season games; **11.6 ppg, 4.3 apg, 2.8 rpg**; 5,687 points, 2,112 assists, 1,290 rebounds.
- **2019 NBA Championship (Toronto Raptors):** Signed after a February 2019 buyout from Atlanta. When Toronto beat Golden State 114–110 in Game 6 (June 13, 2019), Lin became the **first Asian American player to win an NBA championship** and the first Harvard graduate to play in an NBA Finals. He logged only ~27 playoff minutes (and under a minute in the Finals), later speaking openly about feeling the league had "given up" on him in 2019 free agency.
- **Career NBA earnings:** $65,750,412 (Spotrac); endorsement earnings reported at ~$51M by 2019.

#### After the NBA — China & Taiwan
- **Beijing Ducks (CBA), 2019–20:** Contract reported at $3M+/year, among the league's highest-paid imports; averaged **22.3 pts, 5.7 reb, 5.6 ast** and was a CBA Defensive Player of the Year finalist.
- **Santa Cruz Warriors (G League), Jan 2021:** NBA comeback attempt; ~10 games at **19.8 ppg, 6.2 apg, 50.8% FG** — no NBA call-up came.
- **Beijing Ducks again, 2021–22:** Returned June 2021; hospitalized with COVID-19 in Shanghai in September 2021, losing ~20 lbs in isolation.
- **Taiwan:** Signed with the Kaohsiung Steelers (P.LEAGUE+) on January 26, 2023; moved to the **New Taipei Kings** later in 2023. Won the **2024 P.LEAGUE+ championship with Finals MVP honors**, then led the Kings to the **inaugural TPBL championship on June 29, 2025** (beating the Kaohsiung FamilyMart Aquas), going back-to-back with another Finals MVP.

#### Recent (2024–2026)
- **Sept 2, 2024:** Extended his contract with the New Taipei Kings.
- **Feb 2025:** Coached Team G League at the NBA Rising Stars mini-tournament during All-Star Weekend.
- **April 2025:** Won TPBL Player of the Month (repeat winner during the season).
- **June 14, 2025:** Named the **TPBL's inaugural regular-season MVP** (2024–25).
- **June 30, 2025 (reported):** Capped the season with the first-ever TPBL title + Finals MVP.
- **Aug 31, 2025:** Announced **retirement** at age 37 via Instagram/Weibo after a 15-year professional career spanning the NBA, China, and Taiwan.
- **Dec 28, 2025:** New Taipei Kings retired his **No. 7 jersey** in a ceremony billed "Linsanity Forever" (covered into January 2026).
- **Personal:** Revealed in early 2023 that he had quietly been married for about two years (wife's name never disclosed publicly; she is of Filipino descent); the couple welcomed a son in 2024.
- **Social reach:** Instagram (@jlin7) at roughly **2 million followers** (2025 figure).
- **Net worth:** Estimates only — ~$36M, with some 2025 outlets citing ~$40M.

#### Business Ventures & Advocacy
- **Jeremy Lin Foundation** (founded 2011): funds grassroots programs serving low-income AAPI youth, with deliberate bridge-building grants to other communities of color.
- **Esports:** Partnered with Vici Gaming in September 2016 to launch Dota 2's Team VGJ (as brand ambassador); added North American squad VGJ.Storm in 2017; rebranded as **J.Storm** in September 2018 under his ownership. The squad reached The International 2018, finishing 7th and winning over $630,000, before J.Storm ceased North American operations (~2020).
- **Tech investing:** Participated in a $15M Series A for Plays.tv (Feb 2017); invested $4M in NEX Team (the AI shot-tracking app HomeCourt).
- **Anti-Asian racism advocacy:** Became one of the most prominent voices against anti-Asian hate during the 2020–21 surge (Stop AAPI Hate logged 10,905+ incidents from March 2020 to December 2021) — wrote viral essays, spoke after the Atlanta shootings (TIME, 2021), and joined an NBC News town hall on anti-Asian hate with Olivia Munn. Also publicized that he was called "coronavirus" on court during his 2021 G League stint.

#### Notable Quotes
- On retirement (Aug 31, 2025): "I've spent my 15 year career knowing that one day I would have to walk away, and yet actually saying goodbye to basketball today has been the hardest decision I've ever made."
- Same statement: "It's been the honor of a lifetime to compete against the fiercest competitors under the brightest lights and to challenge what the world thought was possible for someone who looks like me." … "I will forever be the kid who felt fully alive every time I touched a basketball."
- On Asian American identity (2021): "We are tired of being told that we don't experience racism, we are tired of being told to keep our heads down and not make trouble… We are tired of the stereotypes in Hollywood affecting our psyche and limiting who we think we can be."
- On responding to hate (2021): "Fighting ignorance with ignorance will get us nowhere. Sharing our own pain by painting another group of people with stereotypes is NOT the way."
- On the famous couch: "It's more comfortable than a lot of beds."
